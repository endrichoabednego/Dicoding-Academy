Museum Affandi

Affandi Museum is located at Jalan Laksda Adisucipto number 167 and occupies an area of 3,500 m2 consisting of a museum building consisting of four galleries along with complementary buildings such as a ticket purchase place, two studios, and a house where the painter Affandi and his family live. Affandi himself, the museum also houses paintings by other Indonesian painters, including: Basuki Abdullah, Popo Iskandar, Sudjojono, Hendra Gunawan, Barli Sasmitawinata, and Mochtar Apin.
Location : Jl. Laksda B Adi Sucipto 167 55281 Depok

Ullen Sentalu

The Ullen Sentalu Museum was inaugurated by the Governor of DI Yogyakarta Sri Paduka Paku Alam VIII on March 1, 1997. "Ullen Sentalu" is an acronym for a philosophy in the Javanese language "Ulating Blencong Sejatining Tataraning Lumaku" which means "light is a guide to the path of life".Ullen Sentalu Museum exists based on inclusiveness and is a space for cultural dialogue from the past, present, and future.
Location : Jl. Boyong Taman Wisata 55581 Yogyakarta Region

Jogja National Museum

Jogja National Museum (JNM) is a contemporary art museum and gallery which is established under the auspices of the Yayasan Yogyakarta Seni Nusantara (YSSN). The exhibition rooms at JNM include: Fine Art Museum Gallery Room, Ajiyasa Hall, Sculpture Site Art Room, and Craft Site Room. JNM provides a special gallery called Gallery for Citizens that can be used by budding artists to hold exhibitions for free. In addition to the showroom, there are also facilities such as the JNM Art Shop, and the Situs Kriya Canteen
Location : Jl. Prof. Ki Amri Yahya no. 1 52281 Yogyakarta Region
